Track and Support Your Child's Development from Birth to 36 Months

Key Areas of Development

Motor Skills
  • 0-3 Months: Moves head, responds to smiling faces
  • 4-6 Months: Grasps objects, smiles at amusing stimuli
  • 6-9 Months: Picks up objects with both hands, may begin to crawl
  • 9-12 Months: Stands with support
  • 18-24 Months: Stacks blocks, starts showing independence
  • 24-36 Months: Scribbles with a pencil, climbs stairs
 Socialization & Independence
  • 0-3 Months: Responds to smiling faces
  • 4-6 Months: Smiles at amusing stimuli
  • 18-24 Months: Calls out "mama" or "papa", shows signs of independence
  • 24-36 Months: Helps tidy up toys
 Speech & Language Abilities
  • 0-3 Months: Responds to sounds
  • 9-12 Months: Makes sounds without meaning
  • 18-24 Months: Starts forming simple words like "mama"
  • 24-36 Months: Points to body parts, engages in more complex language tasks

Why Track Developmental Milestones?

  • Monitor Growth: Ensure your child is progressing as expected.
  • Early Interventions: Identify and address developmental delays early.
  • Support & Guidance: Provide the right activities to nurture your child's growth.
